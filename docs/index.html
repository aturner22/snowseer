snowseer

Constants as the bridge.

Minimal-shot autonomy via geometric correspondence.

15 s clip · Boreas · Toronto · January 2021. Green = the road, transferred from a clear-season prior. Nothing in the pipeline has been trained on snow. (Hero frame above; reproduce the full clip with make reproduce.)

Motivation

When labels can’t keep up

Minimal-shot autonomy is the question of how a perception system survives in regimes it has not been heavily trained on. The default move — collect more data and retrain — does not scale across the long tail. A snow plough drives in a regime that labelled training corpora deliberately exclude; a Cityscapes-trained segmenter applied directly to the snowy frame reports the entire scene as sky.

For almost every operating regime where autonomy fails for lack of data, an adjacent regime exists — temporally, seasonally, geographically — where data is rich, and where the parts that matter are the same. The plough’s road is the same road it was last July. Its appearance has changed completely; its position in space has not.

The principle

The constants-bridge

A constants-bridge is a composition that takes a model trained on regime A, an inference target in regime B, and a known invariant linking the two, and uses the invariant to transfer the model into B without retraining.

The invariant in this work is geometric — the road sits where it sat last summer. The shape is more general: anatomy across imaging conditions, terrain across illumination, scene structure across weather, orbital geometry across polar darkness. The constituent parts (frozen feature matcher + frozen Cityscapes segmenter + classical RANSAC) are not new; the contribution is to identify the composition itself as a primitive worth naming, and to demonstrate it end-to-end on a regime its components have never been trained on.

Watch it work

The canonical clip

15 s, residential street near UTIAS in Toronto. Heavy snow, road buried, lane markings invisible. The strongest matches anchor on gate posts, fence wires, and distant rooflines — features the season can’t change. The mp4 is regenerated by make reproduce; the frame above is the t = 5 s sample extracted by make track TRACK=boreas_2021_01_26.

A second canonical run on the same Toronto loop in different snowfall conditions (boreas_2025_02_15) executes the same code with the same parameters. Evidence the principle is robust to the conditions inside the snow regime, not just to one capture.

Worked example

One frame, four panels

What the matcher actually anchors on, on a representative frame:

Left, snow query; right, summer prior; correspondences drawn between matched features. The lines connect features the season has not changed — gate posts, fence wires, masonry corners, distant rooflines. No correspondences land on the road surface, because the snow has erased the lane markings and the kerb — the matcher knows there is nothing visually consistent there.

The geometry from those non-road constants registers the prior’s road mask back into the snow frame’s pixel coordinates. We are not matching road to road; we are using the constants on the buildings to fix the geometry, then carrying the road across.

Every learned component is frozen. None is fine-tuned on snow. Snow appears only at inference, as the runtime input.

Static-stills precursor

27 pairs across northern Sweden & Finland

The earlier version of the work (make stills) demonstrated the principle on individual snow / clear-season image pairs across twenty-seven different physical locations — the foundation the video extension was built on.

Known limitations

What didn’t work, and where the system ends

  • Synthetic priors from past snow frames — 3-7× more correspondences than snow→summer, but in motion each frame’s slightly-too-large mask seeded the next frame’s prior. The road overlay drifted outward into vegetation. Positive feedback loop. Rejected.
  • Optical-flow propagation — vanishing-point flow on a forward-driving camera stretches the previous mask outward at every step. Same shape, different mechanism. Rejected.
  • Inlier count is not a reliable predictor of overlay quality. Matches concentrated on building façades can warp the road mask onto buildings even when there are many of them. The system needs a human in the loop on input and output.
  • Not real-time on CPU. Matcher-bottlenecked at ~16 s per frame; canonical clip’s cache builds in roughly an hour. A deployment-engineering choice, not a research question; first item in next-steps.

Failure modes that look better in stills and worse in motion are themselves evidence of mechanism. The discipline that emerged: do not call a video result a win from sampled stills.

Where this could go

The constants-bridge as an appliance

The next phase is not “improve the snowplough” — it is to build the general image-banking-and-transfer appliance, of which the snowplough is one consumer.

Live frame + location signal → register against a banked clear-conditions image → transfer any pre-computed annotation into the live frame.

Consumers, beyond snow: fog / dust / smoke / heavy rain / night driving (bank is the clear-conditions capture of the same place); seeing round corners (bank is earlier captures of the upcoming intersection — from this drive or from V2V partners); heads-up display navigation; construction-zone delta detection; industrial inspection in obscured environments (mining, post-fire, decommissioning); agricultural autonomy and drone localisation in featureless terrain (bank is aerial / satellite).

Five v3 items the prize money funds:

  1. Real-time matcher — DISK + LightGlue (or RoMa / LoFTR) on GPU; ~16 s → ~30 ms per frame, required for live operation.
  2. Visual place recognition front-end — replace the GPS-pose lookup so the appliance works in GPS-denied environments.
  3. Multi-source bank — revive the substrate-pluggable layer (archived after our v3 substrate experiment) with a denser cross-domain matcher so amateur Mapillary uploads can substitute for operator captures.
  4. Hardware prototype — battery-powered Jetson-class device with HUD output, demonstrating snow / fog / night-driving consumers end-to-end.
  5. Two more constants-bridge instances for proof of generality — agricultural autonomy and industrial inspection in obscured environments.

Reproduce

From a clean clone

git clone https://github.com/aturner22/snowseer; cd snowseer
uv sync --python 3.12
make reproduce

~6 GB download (Boreas snow + summer windows) plus roughly an hour of matching on Mac CPU. Produces outputs/video/boreas_2021_01_26/overlay.mp4 — the canonical clip whose hero frame is embedded above.

Other entry points: make oracle TRACK=<id> for the pre-flight gate before any new track; make track TRACK=boreas_2025_02_15 for the robustness alt; make stills for the static-stills precursor (needs MAPILLARY_TOKEN); make notebook to re-execute the analysis notebook in place; make pdfs to render writeup.pdf + slides.pdf.
